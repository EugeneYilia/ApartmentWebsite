FAQs

frequently asked questions

  • Do I need a lawyer / solicitor to buy a property?

No, not any more as a legal requirement. However it is highly advisable as solicitors carry out necessary legal property surveys and conduct a property title check going back over 20 years; they ensure the property is free of any mortgage notes, claims, expropriations, rights-of-way and, in general, any legal encumbrances. In addition, solicitors ensure that all property taxes burdening the vendor have been paid.

  • Do I need a notary to buy a property?

Yes. A notary public is a government-appointed lawyer who processes and certifies all real estate transactions, including drawing up and reviewing all official documents, to ensure the legal transfer of the property.

  • Do I need an accountant when buying a property ?

Yes. It is essential to hire an accountant early on to help you with tax returns and explain property taxation laws to you.

  • What type of property insurance is recommended?

Although no market standards exist as to the forms of insurance that can be taken out, it is reasonable to suggest that property be insured against the following risks: fire, earthquake, water damage, breaking glass, storm, theft, and third party.

  • What happens if the legal searches highlight any problems with the property?

If the searches highlight any problems you will receive your deposit back in full. This is why the deposit is paid to the solicitor and not to the vendor.

  • What happens if I change my mind?

If you have signed a contract and paid a deposit for a property, this is considered to be an official intent to purchase. It is important that you are absolutely certain that this is the property you want, and that you feel you are under no pressure to sign a contract and pay a deposit. Should you decide to withdraw from the purchase, you will lose your deposit.

  • Do I need an international bank account?

Yes. All necessary payments associated with the purchasing of the property will be made through this account. This account will also help prove that funds used for the property purchase have come into the country from another country and are, therefore, not taxable in wherever transaction may be taking place. Opening a bank account usually only takes a few minutes and requires no minimum opening deposit. All you need is your ID or passport and your bill with proof of your address. This is different for most countries. We encourage you to research more.

  • Can you walk me through the buying process?

The process is fairly quick and uncomplicated. As a general rule, you must: Select your property. Appoint your solicitor, notary public, surveyor, and accountant. Apply for your Tax Registry Number (AFM) (A Lawyer can do this on your behalf through Power of Attorney). Open a bank account in the country where you buying the property; You will need; A copy of your passport, a recent utility bill, proof of income & your tax number (AFM) Sign the pre-contract detailing terms of sale and payment schedule. Pay a 10% deposit to secure the property by removing it from the market; usual payment methods include cash, bank transfer or credit card. Return to the country of purchase to sign the final contract, after all legal work is completed (4-6 weeks, usually). Alternatively, your solicitor may sign for you if he/she has been granted full Power of Attorney. In order to sign the final contract all fees and taxes must be paid. Be noted however that, this process is however different for different countries.
